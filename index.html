What impact did Tim Berners-Lee have?

Sir Tim Berners-Lee is a British-born computer scientist who is credited for inventing the World Wide Web (a.k.a WWW). Tim originally created the World Wide Web in 1989 for Univerities and other institutes to use as a tool for automated information sharing. The invention of the World Wide Web was one of the most significant advancements in the internet's history, and essentially created the way information is shared today. After creating the Web, Berners-Lee also created the first web browser called WorldWideWeb (later renamed to Nexus) in 1990, which was essentially the only way to view the web. Tim later became the co-founder of his own company, Inrupt, which helps develop an open source program called Solid.


Timeline of his life
  • Tim is born on June 8, 1955 Tim gew up in London, England. His mother and father both worked on the first commerically built computer, the Ferranti Mark I. This sparked Tim's interest in computers at a young age.
  • The Queen's College 1973-1976 Tim went to the Queen's College, Oxford for 3 years and received a Bachelor of Art degree in physics. During his time at the college, Tim made his own computer out of an old television.
  • Tim joins CERN 1980-1984 While working at CERN (The European Organization for Nuclear Research), Tim created his own program called Enquire and pitched his idea for a global hypertext document using the internet.
  • Tim creates the World Wide Web 1989-1990 In 1989, Tim creates the WWW along with its respected web browser and web server. This creation kickstarted the modern computer age was we know it.
  • Present Tim Berners-Lee has since been crowned by Queen Elizabeth II in 2004 for his development of the internet, along with receiving various prestigeous awards such as the Turing award in 2016. Tim now runs a company called Inrupt, along with his co-founder, John Bruce.
